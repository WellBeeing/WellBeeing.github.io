What is IV Hydration Therapy?

Intravenous (IV) Hydration Therapy is a rapid way to replace lost water into the body by direct injection to the bloodstream, usually through a vein in the arm. It is also useful as an aid to balancing micronutrient and electrolyte deficiencies.

Our bodies are 75% water by weight. When we go below a certain threshold we can put the body’s functional capability in grave peril.

Is it Safe?

Yes! Dr. John Myers pioneered of intravenous infusion of vitamins and minerals in the 1970’s, and since then IV Hydration Therapy has shown to be a safe way to restore, replenish and maintain the body’s peak hydration levels. It is generally painless and adverse effects if any are usually mild if administered by a properly trained MEDICAL PROFESSIONAL.

Who can give Hydration Therapy?

In order to receive our services, State Boards of Licensing require specific training and licensing of a Medical Professional to ensure quality of client care. Most States require a Board Certified Physician, Physician’s Assistant or Nurse Practitioner for the administration of IV Hydrotherapy. In some cases, a Registered Nurse (RN), Licensed Practical Nurse (LPN), Licensed Vocational Nurse (LVN) or Emergency Medical Technician (EMT) can administer with appropriate above supervision.
